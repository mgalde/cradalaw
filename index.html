What is a CRADA?

Understanding the framework for federal-private collaboration.

A Cooperative Research and Development Agreement (CRADA) is a formal agreement that allows U.S. government laboratories to collaborate with non-federal entities—including private companies, universities, and nonprofits. CRADAs are used to speed up innovation, share resources, and protect intellectual property while preserving public benefit.

Unlike standard procurement contracts, CRADAs are not about buying services—they’re about building partnerships. They offer a flexible and efficient way to integrate cutting-edge research with real-world applications.

Whether you're a startup seeking lab resources, a university commercializing a discovery, or a government lab needing industry expertise, Sarah Hestad can help craft a CRADA that protects your interests while facilitating progress.
